Literature Survey

This mobile application mainly focuses on assessing the growth of social and emotional, movement and speech of children in low income countries like Sri Lanka. Children are disproportionately affected by the rapidly unfolding economic crisis, rising food and fuel prices, along with frequent power cuts and shortages of life-saving medicine, are particularly impacting the poorest and most marginalized. In 2020, the Family Health Bureau of the Sri Lankan Ministry of Health observed that among children under the age of 5, 8.2% had stunting, 8.6% had wasting, 1.2% had severe wasting (SAM), and 7.5% had moderate wasting(MAM). Thus, it is crucial to examine and keep track of the child’s growth in order to ensure their development. Parents in Sri Lanka typically take their children to the doctor or arrange for a midwife to visit their home to monitor their children’s health and growth. These factors have adversely affected and have pushed the present traditional technique used in Sri Lanka toward a setback. 

The previous study on Establishing New Norms for Developmental Milestones has used 41465 screenings across three states to analyze responses to the developmental screening form of the Survey of Well-being of Young Children. Associations between developmental status and a range of child characteristics were investigated, and norms for individual questions were compared to Centers for Disease Control and Prevention guidelines for the attainment of key milestones (CDC). As a result, the CDC recommendations are more attuned to social, cognitive, and communicative ability deficits . The researchers of Parenting interventions to promote early child development in the first three years of life, conducted research in 33 different countries. They discovered that parenting strategies improved young children’s cognitive, speech, motor, socioemotional, and relational development and decreased behavior issues. In addition, parenting treatments improved parent-child relationships, knowledge, and techniques. Several studies were implemented worldwide to assess the development of children. Baby CROINC and Baby Tracks have used a mobile application to collect data and conduct the questionnaire base analysis. Since child developmental growth differs from region to region, it is essential to have a unique set of milestones tailored for Sri Lankan children. Therefore, this study was conducted through surveys to collect and analyze child development data from hospitals, daycare centers, and wellness clinics.

A machine learning algorithm was trained to predict whether the child reached the relevant milestone or not. Face detection and recognition is a heavily researched topic, and a vast amount of models were already implemented. But most of the studies have used adult image datasets to train the model and do predictions. Based on the context of Sri Lanka, social and emotional milestones between the ages of 2 and 5 were selected for this study. A children’s dataset was used to train the model and predict the facial expression of the child. Human action recognition is a challenging task, and most past researchers have used a variety of approaches. The modern evaluation of Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) and Long-Short Term Memory Network (LSTM) models has reduced the complexity of human action recognition using RGB videos. Most of the previous studies have focused on human action recognition based on adult video datasets. In this study, specific action classes were chosen based on the Sri Lankan context to predict the acts performed by children aged 2 to 5. The Long-Term Recurrent Convolutional Network (LRCN) architecture was selected as the best approach in this study for the action recognition due to its capability to recognize the child’s actions with higher accuracy.

Research Gap

Proper monitoring of a child's development and changes over time and observation, the researchers discovered that a life course conceptual framework of early childhood development presents children at risk of not reaching their developmental potential. When comparing with adult intelligence, several developmental milestones measured during the first three years of life were linked to adult IQ in a large sample. Therefore, proper tracking of child development is essential in human life.

When compared with the CDC website, the milestone that almost all the countries used are similar. But as Sri Lankans, it is essential to have a unique set of milestones which are unique for Sri Lankans. Though there were mobile applications to track child development, most of them asked the parent to add the milestone using text. When considering the user experience, it is more time-consuming. And as the output, it gives a summarized report with the child's milestone percentiles within developmental areas. A checklist of milestones relevant to each milestone was given to overcome this in the proposed mobile application. The parent or the caregiver will be able to tick the tasks by adding a tick.

In addition to the checklist, there will be an automated milestone analyzer where the parents can upload a video with the given task. This will identify the child's emotion, movement, or speech ability. Furthermore, the user will be able to assess their child's development and identify in which percentage they have passed the milestones. Therefore, by using this application, the users can determine whether their child has a developmental disorder.

Research Problem

Developmental delays can be detected by closely observing a child's growth and changes over time, as well as by examining whether the child reaches important developmental milestones in speaking, moving, and behaving in specific ways, learning, and playing. But most of the parents in Sri Lanka need to gain knowledge about the specific milestones the child should achieve at different ages. As a results, the majority of children with developmental disorders go undetected.

Research Objectives

The main objective of this research is to use video recordings and parents’ feedbacks to analyze the social and emotions, movements, and speech of children at different developmental milestones and to assess whether they reach the relevant milestones in their childhood. Based on the prior literature review and the gaps identified, we provide the following significant contributions to this research.

  • Design and develop a model to assess whether the child has reached the relevant milestone. Conduct the assessment for the children using the developed model in the mobile application. 

  • Identify social and emotional responses of the child at their various developmental milestones and to design a deep learning model to assess the milestones using collected video recordings. 

  • Using video recordings, identify the child's movements at various developmental stages and create a deep learning model to assess the movements in each milestone.

  • Identify the child's speech at key developmental stages and create a deep learning model to assess the milestones using video recordings gathered.
